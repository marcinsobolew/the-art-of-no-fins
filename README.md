# ☯️ The Art of No-Fins | Biomechanical & Training Lab

> **CNF / DNF Biomechanical Optimization & Experimental Training Methodology**

## 🎯 Mission & Philosophy

- **Self-Experiment:** Continuous CNF & DNF biomechanical optimization and vector force tracking.
- **The Bridge (Pool to Depth):** Utilizing horizontal pool training (DNF) as a controlled laboratory environment to engineer, test, and lock in high-efficiency movement patterns and muscle memory, which are then transferred directly into vertical depth execution (CNF).
- **The Goal:** Achieve the lowest drag streamline ($C_d \to \min$) and maximum propulsive impulse per kick cycle.
- **The Method:** Testing, recording, and refining no-fins mechanics on myself in the pool before adapting them to compressive depth environments.
- **The Enhancement:** Integrating **Blood Flow Restriction (BFR)** protocols for targeted motoric & metabolic adaptation.

## 🔬 Current Focus: Kick Redefinition Lab

We are currently analyzing and refining the 4 key joint mechanics of the kick cycle:

- **Hips:** Strict $0^\circ$ Hip Lock (*No-Hip-Break* during recovery phase).
- **Knees:** Narrow Track execution. Maximum recovery strictly within the body/shoulder shadow. Controlled knee dispersion permitted only upon ankle eversion.
- **Ankles:** Full eversion ($30^\circ$) coupled with a terminal **Power-Clamp / Whip-Snap** sole finish.
- **Upper Body:** Locked *Arrow Position* with axial head stowage between shoulders.
