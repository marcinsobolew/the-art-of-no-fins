THE ADVANTAGE

Version & Component Key Cue (What You Do) The Advantage (What You Gain)
v1.1 Core
Legs + Timing
Lock Arrow Before the Kick

Never start the backward whip kick drive until your hands & arms are fully locked overhead in a tight arrow position.

Max Glide / Zero Brake

100% of leg power pushes you through a bulletproof streamline instead of slamming into water resistance.

v1.1 Core
Arms + Legs
Delay Knee Bend in Arm Recovery

Slide hands upward toward the Arrow position. Initiate knee bending only when arms reach roughly 2/3 of their recovery path overhead.

Preserves Momentum

Stops your thighs from acting like a wide sea-anchor while hands are sliding upward into the streamline.

v1.1 Core
Arms + Mobility
Backstroke Drills for Scapular Freedom & Flat Body Line

Swim backstroke kicking sets regularly. Use water support to open scapular range of motion while practicing full kick extension into a perfectly straight, flat body line.

Effortless Arrow & Flat Profile

Unlocks thoracic mobility for a strain-free Arrow position while training full kick completion into a completely horizontal, straight side profile.

v1.2 Test
Legs + Feet
Sole Clap Whip Finish

Maintain knees locked at strict shoulder width (v1.1 baseline). Conclude the kick by snapping foot soles together in a terminal whip finish.

Direct Thrust Vector

Shoots water straight backward instead of spilling power sideways into wide drag splashes.

Daily Mobility
Hips + Ankles
"Better less, but daily"

10–15 min micro-dosing of 90/90 rear ankle lifts, W-sits, prone ankle grabs (reverse hollow body), scapular stretches & foam rolling instead of chaotic weekly mega-stretching.

Permanent Neural & Joint Adaptations

Rewires internal hip rotation, ankle eversion, and scapular mobility without overloading tendons or causing pre-session muscle fatigue.

Component 2 • Arms
🟢 Active: v1.0 🧪 Testing: v1.1

2. Arms Pull & High Elbow Catch

Hydrodynamic analysis of forearm lift force, high elbow catch position, propulsion stroke under chest, and testing spread fingers surface area (v1.1).

#arms #high-elbow #spread-fingers-v1.1
Key Arm Mechanics:
  • High Elbow Catch: Forearm angled at $90^\circ$ to stroke direction for max surface area.
  • v1.1 Spread Fingers: Testing micro-gaps between fingers to increase effective propulsive area.
  • Streamlined Recovery: Hands slide upward touching ribs/chest to minimize frontal drag.
Component 3 • Glide 🟢 Active: v1.0

3. Axial Alignment & Glide Phase

Every stroke cycle must start and finish locked strictly along a single central trajectory axis, eliminating lumbar arch drag and keeping the head stowed between shoulders.

#glide #axis-lock #start-finish-line
Axis & Stroke Line Checkpoints:
  • Start & Finish Alignment: Every stroke sequence begins and resets into the exact same straight axial line.
  • Head Stowage: Head tucked neutrally between shoulders, eyes looking strictly at the floor/line.
  • Pelvic Neutral: Core engaged to prevent lumbar arching when transferring momentum into the glide.
Component 4 • Timing 🟢 Active: v1.0

4. Timing & Hydrodynamic Cadence

Initiating the leg kick propulsion (downward drive) precisely when the hands and arms are locked into the streamline arrow position.

#timing #arrow-position #kick-initiation
Key Timing Rule:

The main downward leg kick drive begins only after the arms have reached the locked arrow position overhead, ensuring propulsive force is delivered through a fully streamlined body.
